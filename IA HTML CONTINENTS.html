Firstly, What Are Continents?

Continents are expansive landmasses comprising diverse terrains and ecosystems, separated by natural boundaries such as oceans. These extensive masses of land are
vital components of Earth's geography, shaping global climates and influencing ecological systems. Continents are characterized by their unique geological features
and have been instrumental in shaping the planet's history. They serve as habitats for various forms of life, including diverse flora and fauna. Continents play a crucial role in
influencing weather patterns, ocean currents, and the distribution of resources. Their distinct characteristics make them key contributors to the planet's overall biodiversity
and environmental dynamics.
